I am a software engineer drawn to the quiet depth of backend systems: the parts of software that ask for patience, precision, and a willingness to sit with hard problems until they start to make sense. Lately, I find myself especially interested in Rust, systems thinking, and the craft of building things that are sturdy enough to be trusted.

This blog is a place for the thoughts I want to keep working on in public. Some of them are about engineering. Some are about the slower, stranger work of becoming a clearer person. I try to make each post useful in some way, whether that means offering a practical idea, a sharper question, or a small bit of perspective that stays with you after you leave.

Away from the keyboard, I spend a lot of time in the gym, out on runs, and playing video games. I like things that reward attention over time: strength training, long walks through difficult ideas, code that improves after a second look, and games that make you earn the next room.
